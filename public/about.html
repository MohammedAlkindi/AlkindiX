I build and study systems where ideas become structure and structure becomes clarity. This site is a working archive — a place to collect and refine the systems I study and build over time.

My work sits at the intersection of computation and visual thinking. I approach every problem as a systems question: what are the inputs, constraints, and feedback loops? Whether building machine learning pipelines or composing a photographic series, the underlying method is the same — observe carefully, model rigorously, iterate.

Research interests

Mathematical structure, interpretable machine learning, documentary photography, geometry in natural systems, and the design of tools that help people think more clearly. Part of the Alkindi network of projects exploring systems, computation, and visual thinking.

Photography

A long-term visual study of structure, geometry, and light — from terraced hillsides to urban symmetry. Medium format. Fieldwork across three continents. Commissions available worldwide.

How I work

Operating principles

01

Silence as architecture

Absence is load-bearing. Stillness carries weight. What is left out defines what remains.

02

Emotion as protocol

Regulate, serialize, transmit intact. Feeling is not opposed to rigor — it is the signal.

03

Graceful degradation

When the storm arrives, fall back without failing. Design for failure, not just success.

04

Graph theory as method

If a path exists, find it. If no path exists, rewire the graph and try again.

05

Lossless compression

Every observation preserved with perfect fidelity. No data lost in translation.

06

Pressure as forge

Constraint breeds innovation. Limitation shapes sound. The best work is built under pressure.
